# STRATA FLOORING PRICING GUIDE
## UK Supply and Installation Cost Reference
### Version 1.0 | Strata Premium Flooring Platform | UK Market

---

> **Purpose and Use:** This guide provides realistic UK market pricing ranges for every flooring category — material supply costs, typical installation costs, and total installed costs. Prices are expressed as ranges across budget, mid-range, and premium tiers. All figures are approximate and based on UK market conditions; they should be used to anchor customer conversations and set expectations, not as fixed quotes. Prices vary by region (London and South East typically 20–35% higher than national average), project size (larger areas attract lower per-m² rates), floor condition (poor subfloor condition adds preparation cost), and market conditions. Prices are stated excluding VAT unless otherwise noted.

---

## HOW TO USE THIS GUIDE

**Three tiers are used throughout:**
- **Budget:** Entry-level products; accessible pricing; typically shorter lifespan or lower performance specification
- **Mid-range:** The mainstream residential and light commercial sweet spot; good quality; appropriate for most applications
- **Premium:** High-specification materials; longer lifespan; superior aesthetics or performance; appropriate for quality renovations and commercial specification

**What the costs include:**
- **Supply cost (material only):** The cost of the floor covering product per m²
- **Installation cost:** The labour cost to prepare and install, including basic subfloor preparation (not including major remedial work such as new screed or structural repair)
- **Total installed cost:** Supply + installation combined; the figure most useful for customer budget conversations

**What is not included in installation costs:**
- Major subfloor levelling work (new screed, significant SLC, structural repair): typically £15–50/m² additional
- Subfloor moisture remediation (DPM, tanking): typically £10–35/m² additional
- Skirting board supply and fitting: typically £8–20/linear metre
- Furniture moving and re-placing: typically £50–150 per room
- Old floor covering removal and disposal: typically £5–15/m²

**Key cost drivers:**
- Wear layer: 0.3mm < 0.55mm < 0.7mm < 1.0mm+
- Core: flexible < WPC < SPC
- Format: standard plank < wide plank < herringbone < custom pattern (Amtico/Karndean)
- Brand: generic < Polyflor < Karndean < Amtico (bespoke)
- Surface quality: basic print < EIR with HD print

**Amtico and Karndean:** These premium brands use a specialist installation system (direct glue-down; no click) with specific adhesives and can incorporate bespoke design layouts, inlays, borders, and feature strips. Their supply-only costs are typically £50–£120/m² and their installation costs are higher due to the specialist method.

## 15. REGIONAL PRICE VARIATION

UK flooring prices vary significantly by region. The national average figures used throughout this guide should be adjusted as follows:

| Region | Adjustment from national average |

## 16. PROJECT SIZE AND ECONOMY OF SCALE

Larger projects attract lower per-m² installation rates:

| Project size | Installation rate adjustment |
|-------------|------------------------------|
| Under 20m² | +20–30% (minimum call-out; set-up time amortised over small area) |
| 20–50m² | Standard rate |
| 50–150m² | −5–10% |
| 150–500m² | −10–15% |
| Over 500m² (commercial) | −15–25% |

**The minimum day-rate consideration:** Most floor layers charge a minimum of half a day (£150–£300) regardless of how small the job. A 5m² bathroom floor may effectively cost £50–£80/m² in installation labour due to the minimum charge.

## 18. COMMERCIAL PRICING — KEY DIFFERENCES FROM RESIDENTIAL

### 18.1 Why Commercial Costs Differ

- **Labour rates:** Commercial projects typically use day-rate or contract-rate pricing, not per-m² residential rates
- **Out-of-hours working:** Commercial installations often require evenings, nights, or weekends to avoid disrupting the occupant; premium rates apply (typically +25–50% on standard rates)
- **Programme constraints:** Commercial contracts have penalty clauses for late completion; high-quality contractors charge for the risk
- **Volume:** Large commercial projects (1,000m²+) attract material discounts and lower per-m² rates

### 18.2 Commercial Installation Rate Benchmarks

| Floor covering | Commercial installation rate (day-rate basis) |
|----------------|----------------------------------------------|
| Commercial carpet tile | £12–£20/m² (large area); £18–£28/m² (smaller) |
| Commercial LVT (glue-down) | £15–£25/m² |
| Homogeneous vinyl (with welding) | £18–£30/m² |
| Commercial ceramic/porcelain | £28–£50/m² |
| Resin self-levelling | £18–£35/m² (excluding materials) |
| PU screed | £25–£45/m² (excluding materials) |

### 18.3 Commercial Material Specification Premiums

Commercial products typically cost more than domestic equivalents due to:
- Higher EN ISO 10874 classification (Class 33–34 vs Class 22–23 residential)
- Higher wear layer thickness in LVT
- Fire certification requirements
- Additional testing (castor chair, Vettermann drum)
- Safety/anti-slip rating certification

Commercial vinyl (Tarkett iQ, Altro): £18–£35/m² vs budget domestic vinyl at £4–£10/m²
Commercial carpet tile (Burmatex, Interface, Desso): £20–£50/m² vs residential carpet at £15–£35/m²

## 20. PRICE COMMUNICATION GUIDANCE FOR AI ADVISOR

### 20.1 How to Present Pricing

Always present prices as ranges, not fixed figures. Use the following framing:

**Good:** "For a mid-range engineered wood floor in a standard 25m² living room, you're typically looking at £65–£80 per m² all in — so roughly £1,600–£2,000 for the room, not including any subfloor work."

**Avoid:** "That floor costs £74.50 per m²." (False precision; will be wrong for many customers)

### 20.2 When to Escalate

The AI should recommend a professional in-person quote when:
- The customer's room has unusual dimensions or features
- Significant subfloor remediation is suspected
- The customer is asking about commercial projects over £10,000
- The customer describes a complex heritage or specialist application
- There are access constraints that would affect installation cost significantly

### 20.3 The Subfloor Conversation

Always introduce subfloor preparation costs early in a pricing conversation:
- "The floor covering itself is [price range], but I should mention that subfloor preparation is often an additional cost that people don't initially budget for. If the floor needs levelling or damp-proofing, that can add £10–£40 per m² to the project."
- This prevents the common scenario where a customer budgets for the floor covering only and is surprised by the total bill.

### 20.4 The "Why Is It So Expensive?" Response

When a customer expresses surprise at a price:
- Acknowledge: "I understand it seems like a lot for a floor."
- Explain: "The cost reflects [X material quality / skilled installation / longevity / performance]."
- Reframe with cost-per-year if appropriate: "A good engineered wood floor at £80/m² installed will last 35 years — that's about £2.30 per m² per year, which is actually less than a cheaper laminate that needs replacing every 10 years."
- Offer alternatives: "If the budget is the main constraint, here are some alternatives that would give you a similar look at a lower price..."
